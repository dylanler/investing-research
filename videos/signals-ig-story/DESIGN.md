## Style Prompt

Editorial Bloomberg-terminal-meets-modern-research aesthetic. Warm off-white paper background, charcoal ink. Precise, data-forward, confident. Monospace for tickers and numbers. Serif display for headlines. Restrained palette with one signal blue for conviction and one warm orange for risk/attention. Glitch/scanline texture only on the X-handle reveal. Think Financial Times meets terminal window meets Every.to.

## Colors

- `#F5F1EA` — Surface (warm paper)
- `#151515` — Ink (near-black for text)
- `#6B6660` — Ink secondary (labels, metadata)
- `#0F3DA0` — Signal blue (conviction / long positions)
- `#D67633` — Warm orange (risk / Hormuz alerts)
- `#0B7A4E` — Bull green (used sparingly on directional views)

## Typography

- Display headlines: `"GT Sectra Display", "Source Serif Pro", Georgia, serif` — heavy weight
- Body + monospace: `"JetBrains Mono", "IBM Plex Mono", "SF Mono", ui-monospace, monospace`

## What NOT to Do

1. No pure black (#000) or pure white (#FFF) — use the warm off-white / charcoal instead
2. No full-screen linear gradients (H.264 banding) — use radial or solid with localized glow
3. No stock drop shadows or glassmorphism — keep surfaces flat and paper-textured
4. No generic sans-serif tickers — always monospace with tabular-nums
5. No emoji or decorative icons — only typographic marks (→ ↑ ↓ • ×)
